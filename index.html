Cambridge Carnival International


Sunday, September 7, 2014, Cambridge, MA


Cambridge Carnival International is a colorful and festive celebration that is rooted in African traditions. The highlight of the festival is a grand costume parade accompanied by rich rhythmic musicality promoting all types of cultures can be seen as revelers masquerade through streets of Cambridge in dazzling handmade costumes dancing to the beat of Carnival. The parade starts at 12:30pm sharp on Blackstone Street between Western Avenue and River Street near the Charles River. The parade runs up River Street, passes through Central Square along Massachusetts Avenue, continues up Main Street and ends in Kendall Square near MIT, Cambridge. For more information, please check the event's website.



New England Indonesian Festival



Saturday, September 13, 2014 starting at 11:00am, The Boston Public Library - Central Library in Copley Square, Boston, MA


New England Indonesian Festival is the biggest Indonesian festival in New England. It was held last year for the first time with over 5,000 attendees. To provide you with the chance to be fully immersed in Indonesian culture, they will bring to you traditional Indonesian dance, theatrical performance and music. In addition, authentic Indonesian cuisines will be provided. Don't miss the chance to experience Indonesian culture. The admission is free and no need to print the tickets. Just come and bring yr friends and family. For more information, please check here.



Boston Local Food Festival



Sunday, September 14, 2014, from 11:00am to 5:00pm, Rose Kennedy Greenway, Boston, MA


SBN's Boston Local Food Festival is a deliciously free outdoor event that connects Massachusetts and New England eaters of all backgrounds with the abundance of fresh, nutritious local food choices available close to home. You will find freshly harvested produce and seafood from farmers and fishermen, entertaining demonstrations and competitions by chefs and other food experts, as well as lively local music of many cultural tastes. For more information about this festival, please check the event's website.



Embracing the Journey of Spanish Jewry: An Evening of Music, A Fusion of Traditions



Sunday, September 14, 2014, 5:00pm, VilnaShul-Boston's Center for Jewish Culture, Boston, MA


Culture, Boston, MA From Spain to Morocco, Greece, Turkey, Israel, South America, Mexico, Cuba and the United States, the dispersion of Sephardic Jewry led to a far-reaching exchange of customs and ideas. This year, as the shofar is blown to gather people together, we strive to promote global awareness of the shared customs and traditions of these diverse Christian, Muslim and Jewish communities. For more information about this event and tickets, please visit the event's website here.
